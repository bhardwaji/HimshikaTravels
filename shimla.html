KUFRI

Kufri is a small hill station in Shimla district of Himachal Pradesh state in India. It is located 20 km from the state capital Shimla on the National Highway No.22. The name Kufri is derived from the word kufr meaning “lake” in the local language. It is famous for its trekking and hiking trails. Also, this region seeks the attention of travellers with its nature parks and picnic spots. Some of the top things to do in Kufri have been mentioned below! 1. Encountering Wildlife 2. CHURDHAR

The enchanting Churdhar peak in Sirmaur is one of the highest Peak of Shivalik ranges at a height of 11965 feet. The Churdhar peak happens to be the highest peak in the outside Himalayan range. This peak is also known as Churi-Chandni-Dhar. It is named after Shri Chureshwar or Shirgul Maharaj because locals believe that they lived here in ancient times. The Churdhar Peak trek is the highest peak of outside Himalayan range with a modest elevation of 12,000 ft. This peak is visible from Shimla, Kasauli, and Kufri, owing to its height. At the top of the Churdhar peak, a giant statue of Lord Shiva looks mesmerizing as well as takes you back to the tales of the great Indian epics like Mahabharata and Ramayana. Experience one of the best trekking trail and camping with amazing scenic views and a glimpse of mountains, forest, and lush green valley. Key Points of Churdhar Trek Level: Easy -Moderate 1. Proper conditioning & training of body required 2. Need proper gear & clothing for sub-zero temperature? 3. Shoes are the single most important equipment. 4. High Altitude requires proper acclimatization. 5. Beautiful landscapes so carry good photo gear & Extra battery backup. 6. Travel light 7. The backpack should not exceed 10 kgs.

NALDEHRA

Naldehra a stunning hill spur that overlooks dense forests of cedars and deodars is located at distance of 22 km away from Shimla in Himachal Pradesh State. Naldehra is situated at a distance of 7 km from Mashobra. A heaven for tourist, Naldehra has derived its name from the two words, Nag and Dehra, meaning the Abode of the King of Snakes. The beauty of Naldehra is added by the meandering of the enthralling River Sutlej. On a clear day, one can view the snow-capped Himalayan Mountains. A paradise for nature lovers, Naldehra is famous as one of the oldest golf courses in the country, located at a height of 2,200 m above sea level. An 18-hole course, it is considered as one of the most challenging in India.
